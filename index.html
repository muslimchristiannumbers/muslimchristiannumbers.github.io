Islam

The most defensible source-backed figure for today is about 2.0 billion Muslims, based on Pew Research Center’s 2025 report that the number of Muslims grew to 2.0 billion by 2020. A 2025–2026 display of 2.0–2.1 billion should be clearly labeled as an estimate, not a direct official count.

Pew’s projections place Muslims at 2.2 billion by 2030 and about 2.8 billion by 2050. A mid-2030s figure of around 2.4 billion can be presented only as an interpolated estimate between those projection points.

Source anchors: Pew Research Center (2025 report on global Muslim population change, and Pew’s projection studies).

Christianity

Gordon-Conwell’s Status of Global Christianity 2026 says the world has about 2.6 billion Christians in 2026, and its 2026 table projects 3.676 billion Christians by 2050.

Gordon-Conwell’s older 2015 projection also stated 3.3 billion Christians by 2050. Because the methodologies differ, the safest way to present this is as a range or by explicitly naming the source used.

Source anchors: Gordon-Conwell Center for the Study of Global Christianity (2026 table) and the 2015 IBMR projection.

Why sources disagree

Different datasets use different methods: census data, surveys, denominational counts, migration adjustments, fertility assumptions, and religious switching. That is why some sites show different totals for the same year.

To stay trustworthy, this page labels every number as either a current estimate or a projection and names the source beside it.

Regional notes

Gordon-Conwell’s 2026 materials note the continuing shift of Christianity toward the Global South. Their 2026 table shows the share of all Christians in the Global South rising from 66.6% to 82.0% in the long-range column.

For Muslims, Pew’s country-level projections show the fastest long-term growth comes from demographic momentum and age structure, not from unsupported one-line claims.
